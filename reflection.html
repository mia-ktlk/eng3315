Entropy

Embrace the tangle

Every task, every moment, every feeling weaves into a tangle. Some feelings and some moments exist on multiple threads. I can be happy and sad at the same time. I can love someone and still dislike almost everything they do. Disagree with their values and bubble over when they speak.

I was taught to search for order in everything. There is always a right or wrong. In science there are protocols. Concrete steps to follow. In school there are grades. GPA: everything neatly represented as one number. In reality, life is not so neat. Why do we pretend that is?

Schedules, planners, Google Calendar, checklists… All tools to untangle and experience the world as flat. But I know that the more you represent the likeness of something the more it becomes an illusion - a distant reflection of what is meant to represent. People joke about living in the Matrix, but I think it’s true. How is living in a fake copy of our lives any different from being plugged into a simulation?

We don’t talk about things that matter - things that are scary. Mental health, death, trauma… pretend like its not there. Only think about it at night, when you’re alone, when no one can hear you scream. The next morning cover up the sleeplessness on your face with makeup. Like it never even happened. Building up the fake world around us. A copy of a copy of our lives.

What if we tried. Just once to talk about the things that scare us. We could realize we aren’t alone. Embrace the tangle.

It’s funny how acknowledging life is full of random terrifying chaos can actually help the threads unravel. Swim in the entropy.
